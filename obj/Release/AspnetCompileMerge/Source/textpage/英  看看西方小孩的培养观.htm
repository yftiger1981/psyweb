Research data showthat multi-age play groups are common in simple socieites made up ofcommuication with fewer than 1,500 people . in contrast ,complex industrialsocieties are more likely to have same age groups that are made up of childrenwho are eaual in age.

In most of the world’s traditional, nonCwestern cultures, chidren paly in groups with genders , ranging in age fromlate infancy to adolescence. In multi-age groups , younger children have aopportunity to learn language, survival skills, and games from children aheadof them in the prcoess of development. The older children get experience inteaching what they know and a chance to feel important and responsible.

In western culture, on the other hand ,parentsand teachers generally believed that is best for children to study and playwith other children of the same age. Thus same-age peer groups are the norm forboth in and out of shool, and at all ages, including infancy. Peers play an importantpositive role in children’s socialization because peers are both equals and competites.In childhood and adolesence, peers are usually friends,and peers groups bulidfriendship,tolerence ,and cooperaton. Peels are status equals in term of power;they can tease and tolerate each other without the intervention of adults.Withpeers children create worlds of meaning in which they become active agents intheir own socializaion. SameCage peer groups promote cooperation and groupidentity .They aslo promote the learning of competitive strategies that arehealthy and necessary for survival in a competitive society.
