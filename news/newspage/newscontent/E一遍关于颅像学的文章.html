  小编：一篇关于18世纪末，奥地利专家骨像学说分析人格特性的文章，不同的头骨形状也有预示着不同的人类性情特征。在19世纪早期欧洲等各国专家也在颅像学方面有所研究，逐渐形成一套系统理论。感兴趣的你是否能在你的朋友中也找到相应的规律？

    In the late eighteenth century, an Austrian physician named Franz JosephGall observed a relationship between certain characteristics and theshapes of people’s head. Gall founded the discipline called phrenology, whichis based on the variations in the human skull reflect differences in the sizeof the human brain. According to phrenology ,difference areas of brain serve separate and distinct functions; therefore, by carefully examining the size andshape of an individual ‘s skull, an expert can determine the strengths,weaknesses, and peculiarities of the persons’ characters and intellect.

    Individuals call uponvisual-spatial intelligence in the reinfoce the use of objects, both when theobjects are seen in their original setting and also when some part of orignialsetting had changed. Visual spatial skills are utilized when an individualworks with graphic depictions –two dimentional or three dimentional versions ofreal –world scenes-as well as other symbols ,such as maps,diagrams, orgeometrial forms.

    Devoting most of his life to a detailed study of the nervous system. Gall showed that the brain’s white matter consists of nerve fibres. He also initiated the theory of localization the belief that thevarious mental processes are centred in specific parts of the brains. Gallclaimed that humans have different forms of power for each mental process. Hedeveloped a list of “organs” of the mind that featured 37 different mentalpowers. Among these powers were emotional faculties such as secret iveness and capability for love; sentiments such as hope ,reverence , and self-esteem; and reflective and perceptual powers, including aptitude for language; musicalability , and sensitivity to visual properties such as shape and color .

  The simple doctrineof phrenology achieved great popularity in Europe and United States during theearly nineteenth century. Many leading scientist of the period supported itsbasic principle and attempted to advance it as a science. The doctrine also appearedto the general public since everyone could “play the game ”of reading skulls.
